# User Interview Research

## Purpose

The goal of these interviews is to understand:

- How teams currently spend on AI tools
- Common frustrations
- Hidden inefficiencies
- Trust expectations for financial recommendations

These interviews will help improve:

- Audit recommendation quality
- Product positioning
- UX decisions
- Credibility

---

# Target Users

## Primary Audience

- Startup founders
- CTOs
- Engineering managers
- Technical team leads

## Secondary Audience

- Freelancers
- Small development agencies
- AI-heavy SaaS startups

---

# Interview Goals

The interviews should uncover:

- Actual spending habits
- Emotional frustrations
- Workflow inconsistencies
- Unused subscriptions
- Trust concerns
- Purchasing behavior

---

# Core Interview Questions

## AI Tool Usage

1. Which AI tools does your team currently use?
2. Which AI tools are paid subscriptions?
3. Approximately how much do you spend monthly on AI tools?
4. Who manages AI subscriptions in your company?

## Workflow Questions

5. Do team members actively use every subscription?
6. Have you noticed overlap between different AI tools?
7. Which tools feel most valuable?
8. Which tools feel unnecessary or underused?

## Financial Awareness

9. How do you currently track AI spending?
10. Do you monitor API token usage regularly?
11. Have AI costs increased recently?
12. Do unexpected charges happen often?

## Trust & Product Validation

13. What would make you trust an AI spend audit platform?
14. Would automated cost-saving recommendations be useful?
15. Would you prefer:

- Automated recommendations
- Manual reports
- Monthly monitoring

---

# Important Research Signals

During interviews, pay close attention to:

- Contradictions
- Emotional complaints
- Messy workflows
- Unclear ownership
- Subscription confusion

These insights are more valuable than generic answers.
